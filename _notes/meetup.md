# Contractor Website Consultation Outline

*Simple, natural, easy-to-follow reference*

---

## 1. Start the Conversation

**Goal**  
Understand their business and what they need most.

**Ask**
- What services do you want to highlight most right now?
- What type of customers do you want more of?
- What cities or areas do you serve?
- What kinds of jobs are most profitable for you?
- What problems are customers calling you about most?

**Example**  
> “Before we talk design, I want to understand where your business is at and what kind of jobs you want more of.”

---

## 2. Identify Their Main Goal

**Ask**  
What do you want the website to do for you?

**Common Answers**
- More phone calls
- More quote requests
- Better leads
- Show professionalism
- Compete locally
- Get more reviews
- Hire workers

**Example**  
> “If this website worked perfectly, what would it help your business do?”

---

## 3. Build Trust Messaging

**Ask**
- What makes you different from other contractors nearby?
- How long have you been in business?
- Are you licensed/insured?
- Any warranties?
- Fast response times?
- Family-owned?
- Any certifications?

**Example**  
> “People compare contractors quickly online, so we want to make trust obvious right away.”

---

## 4. Content They Need

**Ask**  
Do you already have:
- Logo
- Photos of past work
- Reviews
- Service list
- Contact info
- Social media links
- Existing website access

**Example**  
> “The more real photos and reviews we use, the stronger the website feels.”

---

## 5. Recommend a Simple Website Structure

**For Most Contractors**

### One-Page Scroll Site (Suggested Sections)

- Hero section
- Services
- Why choose us
- Gallery
- Reviews
- Service areas
- FAQ
- Call to action
- Contact form

**Example**  
> “Most people just want fast answers and an easy way to call you. A clean one-page site works great.”

---

## 6. Lead Generation Focus

Explain: Website should turn visitors into calls.

Include:
- Call-now buttons
- Quote request form
- Mobile-friendly layout
- Clear contact info
- Multiple calls-to-action

**Example**  
> “We’re not building a brochure—we’re building something that gets calls.”

---

## 7. Local SEO Talking Points

**Ask**
- Which cities do you want to target most?
- Which services matter most?

Explain: We can help show up better locally.

**Example**  
> “If someone searches your service in your city, we want you positioned to compete.”

---

## 8. Tone / Branding

**Ask**  
How do you want people to feel when they land on your site?

Options:
- Professional
- Friendly
- Reliable
- High-end
- Fast-response
- Honest / straightforward

**Example**  
> “We want the site to sound like you—not robotic or corporate.”

---

## 9. Pricing Framework (Internal Reference)

- Simple Scroll Site — $1,500+
- Strong Lead Gen Site — $2,500+
- SEO / Growth Build — $3,500+

Monthly options:
- Hosting / Maintenance
- SEO
- Ads
- Updates

---

## 10. How To Explain Value

Don't Say:
- “It’s one page”
- “It’s 5 pages”

Say:
- Designed to generate calls
- Builds trust fast
- Helps local visibility
- Easy for customers to contact you
- Professional online presence

**Example**  
> “This is a business tool that helps bring in jobs.”

---

## 11. Strong Closing Questions

- What’s the biggest thing missing from your current online presence?
- If we launched this in 30 days, what would success look like?
- Would you like something simple and effective, or more growth-focused?

---

## 12. Closing Statement Example

> “Based on what you shared, I’d recommend a clean contractor website focused on trust, local visibility, and getting more calls.”

---

## 13. Quick Consultation Flow (Use This Every Time)

1. What do you do?  
2. What jobs do you want more of?  
3. What area do you serve?  
4. What makes you different?  
5. Do you have photos/reviews?  
6. Need more calls or more credibility?  
7. Want simple one-page or growth-focused site?  
8. Present recommendation  
9. Quote project  
10. Close next step

---

## 14. Remember This

Contractors usually care about:
- More calls
- More jobs
- Trust
- Looking professional
- Keeping it simple

Not fancy web terms.

---

## 15. Personal Reminder

Keep it simple. Guide the conversation. You do not need to know everything upfront. Just solve business problems clearly.
